Marlon Brando

April 3, 1924 - July 1, 2004

Marlon Brando, Jr. was an American actor, film director and political activist. He is credited with bringing realism to film acting. He helped to popularize the Stanislavski system of acting, studying with Stella Adler in the 1940s.

Short Timeline

Apr. 3, 1924: Born as Marlon Brando, Jr. in Omaha, Nebraska to Dorothy Pennebaker and Marlon Brando, Sr.

Mar. 31, 1954: His mother dies.

Apr. 2, 1958: Plays a Nazi officer in Young Lions.

Mar. 24, 1972: Plays Don Vito Corleone in Francis Ford Coppola's The Godfather with James Caan and Al Pacino.

July 1, 2004: Marlon dies of lung failure at the UCLA Medical Center. Some of his ashes are spread over Death Valley.
